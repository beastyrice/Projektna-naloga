        The UK Official UK Charts (Singles Chart). In my opinion, they’ve completely lost their way. It used to be about buying a single and excitedly hoping or seeing it climb the charts. Now, 'album' tracks flood the singles charts partly thanks to streaming but more because of the rules, even if the song was never released as single it can be included in the chart. It’s why artists like Taylor Swift, Billie Eilish, and Sabrina Carpenter dominate the charts with 'album' tracks. My view? Only officially released singles / tracks should be eligible, and album streams/downloads should count separately and not be included. Separate track with separate artwork and catalogue number and an official release date.

        The current system for radio and charts isn’t just frustrating—it’s unfair to the listeners and to artists working hard to bring fresh music to the world.
