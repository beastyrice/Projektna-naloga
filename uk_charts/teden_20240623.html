        NEW Chart Review 21/06/24

        Oh, hey. I'm on holiday. Couldn't you guess by how late I've uploaded this? Only took me a couple of hours. 😓

        As you can probably tell, this review is gonna be a little shorter today. And next week. And from now on, actually. You see, after the OCC decided to backhand me across the face when posting my rankings for the last couple of weeks, I've chosen to redesign my reviews somewhat. Starting from this week, here's the order of how I'll do all this risky business:

        Opening noodlings, No.1 singles and album chart talk, other Top 40 new entries, chart movements elsewhere, the rest of the newbies, next week's hopefuls, A NEW RANKING SYSTEM, my thoughts on every new Top 100 hit this week, and P.S. message.

        Got it? Good. I'll end up forgetting by August anyway. And the album chart is as horrible as always. Does it look like I want to talk about The Tortured Poets Department again? Because I don't. At least her touring like crazy has meant that Fortnight sneaks back into the Top 10 again. That's nice, especially because I can't get it out of my head due to Radio 1 jamming it in my brain every hour.

        But that's not important. The No.1 single this week though, IS.

        Two weeks is all Eminem is capable of managing, I'm afraid, as Houdini gets whipped down to 3rd place to make way for the great Sabrina Carpenter invasion. Even though her album isn't out 'til August. What's more interesting is the fact that her old chart-topper Espresso hasn't regained pole position. New single Please Please Please has ended up at the summit. It's a pretty odd set of circumstances that's come along with the public's swift change of preferance, even though you'd think Espresso would be No.1 due to the scorching hot weather outside. Turns out we're all wrong as Please Please Please sounds like something Miley Cyrus would make before indulging in her 2015 prog-rock escapades, only it's more pop-orientated. And better. Not as good as her other omnipresent smash but there you go. THIS is the song of the summer, no matter how many faded singers from defunct pop groups tell you otherwise.

        As for the rest of the Top 40 and it seems I'm not the only one to go on holiday this week. The chart's decided to pack it's bags and deport itself to Antarctica for a week, meaning we've ended up with more re-entries than new entries (although I don't think Disturbed's The Sound of Silence really counts, does it?). Thus, I will officially grant my honest praises to Chappell Roan for upsetting the applecart and nicking a 2nd Top 40 hit with HOT TO GO!, which is basically just one long High School Musical cheerleader chant set to music. Only a lot more manageable than HSM. And sadly not played on Radio 1's Chart Show. It thus joins Good Luck, Babe in the upper regions, and with Red Wine Supernova also in the current Spotify Top 50 (last time I checked anyway), you can bet your life she won't be going away in a hurry. Unlike the song involved in this next paragraph.

        Now as you know, whenever a football tournament occurs, something very peculiar happens to the British public. They go out and stream Three Lions. Originally conceived as an anthem for drunken dads to gurgle along to in 1996 by comedians David Baddiel and Frank Skinner, along with Britpop chuggers The Lightning Seeds, it has of course become synonymous with the England team in internatio football. And it re-enters every single time they play in a tournament. I still have PTSD flashbacks from that day in 2018 when poor George Ezra had to play second fiddle to a song that was only marginally better than his tripe. But I digress. It all kickstarts again this year as Three Lions pops up at 32, but try not to get confident guys. Have you seen the England team in the Euros this year? I'm surprised Gareth Southgate hasn't spontaneously combusted on the spot yet.

        Elsewhere in the Top 100, Tommy Richman is a potential 1-hit wonder, his followup to MILLION DOLLAR BABY entering at a less than satisfactory 48, Byron Messia fares little better, Jordan Adetunji does alright for himself at 55, a song called Kisses has risen a lot to 58, a Kid LAROI album track pops up in the 60s, Luke Combs spawns a couple borers from his album, Pozer doesn't do very well, Dominic Fike has little luck, Meghan Trainor also gets a couple of newbies, some guy called Adam Port appears for the first time, the guy who "remixed" that Disturbed song is here, and cassö does some 90 places worse than his previous single.
